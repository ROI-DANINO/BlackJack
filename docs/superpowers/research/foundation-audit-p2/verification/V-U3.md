### Ruled: `Revise` vs `Relabel` — K-U3-001 (`:285`)

**`Revise` upheld; it is not a `Relabel`.** The distinction turns on whether the sentence *asserts*
something its basis does not support. `:285` reads verbatim: "Exact values are research-calibrated
configuration rather than fixed architectural assumptions." The assertoric content is a claim about
the **provenance** of the values, in the present tense. The project's own same-day approved baseline
states the opposite about exactly these values — `...product-activity-research.md:287` (ALR-027
limitation, verbatim): "numeric values require calibration and may vary by accessibility/interaction
mode; the research does not establish universal minutes." A sentence saying the values *are*
calibrated, against a basis saying the research *does not establish* them, overstates its basis.
That is the definition of `Revise`. The examiner's mitigation ("no number is printed at `:285`") does
not convert it to a `Relabel`: the overstated object is the provenance, not any printed figure, and a
provenance claim is false-or-not independently of whether a number appears. The examiner reached the
right verdict on a weaker ground than the one available to it (see C-U3-005).

### Ruled: `Revise` vs `Relabel` — K-U3-002 (`:412`)

**`Revise` upheld; it is not a `Relabel`,** and it is correctly judged the more consequential of the
two. `:412` reads verbatim: "Thresholds and retention intervals are versioned, research-calibrated
curriculum/reducer policy." The same baseline states, verbatim at `:317` (ALR-032 limitation):
"counts, diversity rules, recency, and retention intervals remain provisional until observed
blackjack data supports them." "Provisional until observed blackjack data supports them" and
"research-calibrated" are not two labels for one epistemic state; the second asserts a property the
first denies. `Revise`, not `Relabel`.

However, the **asymmetry the record uses to separate the two verdicts is overstated** — see
C-U3-002. The separation survives, but on grounds (b) and (c), not on ground (a) as written.
